## TL;DR — the corrections that matter

1. **Machine is the EMV 401**, not the "EMV 409" the assistant currently names. Texatherm's detailed public specs (incl. the 300 m remote) are published for the **EMV 409**; there is **no public EMV 401 spec sheet**. Attribute model-specific numbers carefully.
2. **Drop "76 dB / quieter than a vacuum."** Texatherm's own TC 170 page lists **63 dB(A)**, and the "twice/four-times quieter" comparison is unsubstantiated. *(Refuted 0–3.)*
3. **Drop the unqualified "WoolSafe approved."** Texatherm is **absent from the WoolSafe directory**, and WoolSafe **does not certify systems, machines or methods at all** — only individual chemical products and accredited service-provider companies. This is the **highest-liability** claim and it is **live in the assistant now.**
4. **"80% less water / 30–60 min drying / exothermic reaction draws dirt from the base of the fibre"** = single-source manufacturer marketing. Attribute it ("Texatherm states…"), present as low-moisture advantages, and **do not assert the exothermic mechanism as science.**
5. **BS EN 1040 is real but narrow** — substantiated by Texatherm for **Dio-Cleanse and Advanced Cleaner** only (with EN 1276 as the suspension-test method). EN 1040 is a *phase-1 basic bactericidal screen under clean conditions* (and the 2005 edition is superseded by EN 13727). Describe it precisely; do not inflate to "kills 99.9% on your carpet."

## Equipment (verified, manufacturer-primary)

### Texatherm TC 170 (the "spinner")
Gear-driven **low-speed rotary, 170 RPM** — 420 mm brush / 406 mm pad, 50 g/cm² brush pressure, 56 Nm torque, 1000 W, 38 kg (44 kg operating). Rated by Texatherm for **"Carpets, Tile, Natural Stone, PVC, Linoleum and other flooring."** 170 RPM is canonical low-speed-rotary territory (bonnet/encapsulation cleaning + hard-floor scrubbing) — **not** a high-speed burnisher. **Noise: 63 dB(A)** per the manufacturer's own page. Source: texatherm.com/product/tc170/.

### Texatherm EMV 401 (the owner's low-moisture/extraction machine)
The owner has confirmed the **EMV 401**. Texatherm publishes a detailed page for the **EMV 409** (full transceiver remote — heat/pump/vacuums — "from up to 300 metres away") and says the remote "comes as standard on all EMV machines." But there is **no public EMV 401 spec sheet** carrying the 300 m / noise / water / drying figures, so those must be attributed to "the Texatherm EMV system (EMV 409 spec)", not pinned to the 401 as verified fact. Sources: texatherm.com EMV 409 + Remote Transceiver Pack pages.

## The method — what we *can* say (independently supported)

- Texatherm is a **low-moisture system**. The defensible, science-backed USP is the **fibre-safety rationale**, not the manufacturer's performance numbers:
  - **Wool and natural fibres (sisal, seagrass, coir, jute) are vulnerable to over-wetting** — shrinkage, browning, cellulosic yellowing, backing delamination, and mould risk if they dry too slowly. Low-moisture methods materially reduce that risk. This is settled fibre science (IICRC-aligned) and is our strongest *accurate* differentiator.
  - **Residue science is real:** detergent/residue left in the pile accelerates re-soiling; a thorough rinse / low-residue approach genuinely keeps carpet cleaner for longer (independent practitioner sources: Cleanfax, HydraMaster).
- **Stain limits to communicate honestly (IICRC):** chlorine-bleach colour loss is **permanent** — correctable only by re-dyeing or patching, never by cleaning. Un-rinsed oxygen ("Oxy") bleach spotters cause equal damage. **Olefin/polypropylene is the one bleach-proof fibre** (solution-dyed). By the same oxidation/dye-destruction logic: old pet-urine dye stains, dried emulsion paint, and heat-set blood are frequently permanent — say so up front.

Product pages omit pH figures.
- **"pH-neutral, no sticky residue"** — manufacturer system-level claim only; **unverified at product level** (needs the SDS PDFs). Attribute, don't assert.

## What to avoid claiming (the cut list)

- ❌ "Our Texatherm system is **WoolSafe approved**." (Absent from the directory **and** outside what WoolSafe certifies — false-certification risk.)
- ❌ "**76 dB** / quieter than a vacuum / four times quieter than a truck-mount."
- ❌ The **EMV 409** model number for ICC's machine (it's the **401**).
- ❌ Stating **80% less water**, **30–60 min drying**, or the **exothermic "draws dirt from the base"** mechanism **as fact** — only as "Texatherm states…".
- ❌ "Kills 99.9% of bacteria **on your carpet**" off the back of EN 1040 (it's a clean-conditions lab screen).
