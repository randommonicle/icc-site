## The right method for your carpet

Period and town-centre homes often have wool, Axminster or Wilton carpets, while many newer properties have synthetic or natural-fibre flooring. We identify the fibre and pile construction before we start, then clean with a low-moisture method that gets carpets genuinely clean while avoiding the shrinkage, browning and slow drying that over-wetting can cause. It suits delicate wool and natural fibres particularly well, and it means your rooms are usable again quickly.

## Honest about what's achievable

We always tell you up front what a clean can and cannot do. Fresh spills and general soiling respond very well; some older staining and any bleach damage is permanent, and we would rather say so before we start than disappoint you on the day. Our [care guides](/guides) explain which stains lift and which do not.
