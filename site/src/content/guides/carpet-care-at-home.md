Good carpet care is not all about the professional clean. A great deal of what keeps a carpet looking its best, and what stops a small accident becoming a permanent mark, is what you do in the first few minutes at home. None of this replaces a proper deep clean, but it is the difference between a spill you forget about and a stain you live with. Here is what you can safely do yourself, and where it is wiser to stop and call us.

## Act fast: the difference between a spill and a stain

While a spill is still wet, it is just a spill, and most come out completely if you deal with them quickly. Leave it, and it dries, bonds to the fibre, and becomes a stain, which is far harder and sometimes impossible to remove. Almost everything you can do at home comes down to one principle: get to it fast, and treat it gently.

## How to treat a fresh spill, step by step

1. **Lift the solids first.** Gently scoop or scrape any solid material with a spoon or blunt knife, working inwards so you do not spread it.
2. **Blot, do not rub.** Press a clean, white, absorbent cloth or paper towel onto the spill to soak it up. White matters, because a coloured cloth can transfer its own dye into the carpet.
3. **Work from the outside in.** Start at the edge of the spill and move towards the middle, so you shrink it rather than spreading it.
4. **Add a little cool water.** Dampen, do not soak, the area with cool water and keep blotting. Cool, never hot: heat can permanently set protein spills such as blood, milk, and egg.
5. **Be patient.** Repeat the dampen-and-blot cycle several times. Most of the work is done by repetition, not force.
6. **Dry it out.** Once you have lifted as much as you can, place a thick pad of dry paper towel over the area, weigh it down with something heavy, and leave it to draw up the last of the moisture.

## What not to do

- **Do not rub or scrub.** It drives the spill in and damages the pile.
- **Do not use hot water** on protein spills like blood, milk, or egg, which it can set permanently.
- **Never use household bleach.** It destroys the carpet's colour and the damage cannot be cleaned out. See our guide on [which carpet stains are permanent](/guides/which-carpet-stains-are-permanent/).
- **Go easy on supermarket stain removers.** Many are too harsh or leave a residue. If you use one, test it on a hidden corner first and follow the instructions exactly.
- **Do not over-wet the carpet,** especially wool or natural fibres, where too much water causes shrinkage and browning. See our guide on [cleaning wool carpets safely](/guides/cleaning-wool-carpets/).

## A simple spill kit

You do not need special products. A small kit kept to hand turns a panic into a two-minute job:

- A few clean white cloths, or a roll of plain white paper towel
- A spoon or blunt knife for lifting solids
- Cool water
- Optionally, a single drop of clear, uncoloured washing-up liquid for greasy marks, blotted out thoroughly afterwards

## Everyday care that prevents most problems

The best stain is the one that never sets, and the easiest dirt to remove is the dirt that never arrives:

- **Vacuum regularly.** Dry grit is abrasive and does most of the long-term damage. Removing it weekly keeps the pile in good condition.
- **Use doormats,** and consider a shoes-off rule. Most soil walks in from outside.
- **Deal with spills immediately,** as above.
- **Rotate rugs and move furniture** occasionally to even out wear and fading.
- **Keep strong, direct sunlight off the carpet** where you can, as it fades colour over time.
- **Tackle pet accidents straight away.** Old, dried urine is one of the hardest things to put right.

## When to stop and call a professional

Home care is about protecting your carpet and limiting damage, not deep cleaning. There is a point where carrying on does more harm than good. It is time to call a professional when:

- the spill is large, or has soaked through into the underlay
- the mark has already dried or set
- it is a valuable, antique, or delicate carpet
- you have tried gentle blotting and it simply is not shifting
- you are not sure what the fibre is or what is safe

Whatever you do, do not keep scrubbing in the hope it will lift, because you risk setting the stain or damaging the pile. And even a carpet that looks clean holds grit and residue deep in the pile that no vacuum can reach, which is why a periodic professional clean keeps it genuinely fresh and lasting longer.
