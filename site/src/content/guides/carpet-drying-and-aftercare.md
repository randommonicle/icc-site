A carpet clean is not finished the moment the machine switches off. How the carpet dries, and how it is looked after in the days afterwards, makes the difference between a clean that lasts and one that disappoints within weeks.

## What affects drying time

- **The method.** This is the biggest factor. A low-moisture clean uses far less water than traditional hot-water extraction, so it dries much faster. Texatherm states a typical drying time of 30 to 60 minutes for its system; a soaked carpet from an over-wet clean can take many hours.
- **The fibre and pile.** Dense, deep or wool pile holds more moisture than a low synthetic pile.
- **Airflow and temperature.** A warm, well-ventilated room dries far quicker than a closed, cold one.
- **How soiled it was.** Heavier soiling needs more cleaning, and sometimes more moisture, to lift.

## Helping it dry safely

- **Move air through the room** — open windows, or use a fan, to speed evaporation.
- **Add gentle warmth** if the room is cold, but there is no need to crank the heating up high.
- **Keep furniture off** the carpet until it is dry. If pieces must go back, use protectors under the feet so metal castors or wood finish cannot mark a damp carpet.
- **Keep traffic light** and step carefully onto any adjoining hard floors.

## Why low residue keeps a carpet cleaner for longer

A carpet that re-soils quickly is usually carrying cleaning-product residue. Detergent left in the pile stays tacky and pulls dirt straight back out of the air and off shoes. A thorough clean that rinses well and leaves little residue genuinely stays cleaner for longer — one of the real, defensible advantages of a careful low-moisture clean over a rushed, over-wet one.

## Longer-term aftercare

- **Vacuum regularly** to lift dry grit before it grinds into the pile.
- **Use doormats** to catch soil at the door before it reaches your carpets.
- **Deal with spills promptly** — our [home care guide](/guides/carpet-care-at-home) covers exactly how.
- **Plan a routine clean** before a carpet looks heavily soiled; maintenance cleaning is gentler and cheaper than rescuing a neglected carpet.
